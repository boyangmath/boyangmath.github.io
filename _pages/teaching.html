Xiamen University

Fall 2020-2024: Complex Analysis (a graduate course)

It is primarily a qualifying-level course for first year graduate students. We also include some advanced topics if time permits.
The topics are changed year by year, accmulatively it covers
(1) Estimates and constructions of holomorphic function, such as Hadamard, Jensen, Weierstrass, Mittag-Leffler, and else.
(2) Special functions, including Gamma and zeta.
(3) Proof of Riemann Mapping Theorem.
(4) Examples of conformal mappings, including Schwarz-Christoffel formula and hypergeometric functions.
(5) Ahlfors-Schwarz lemma and its application to Picard's theorems.
(6) The d-bar equation in complex dimesion one.
(7) Elementary aspects on holomorphic functions of several complex variables.
Lecture notes are under constant revision, and hopefully will be posted soon.

Spring 2023: Complex manifolds

It is a graduate topic course on complex differential geometry.
Our goal was to cover first two chapters of Székelyhidi's book "An introduction to extremal Kähler metrics". Click here for the course calendar and HW assignments.

Spring 2018-2023, 2026: Complex Analysis (an undergraduate course)

It is for second year math majors.
It covers elementary complex analysis of a single complex variable.
(1) The main textbook is Jiarong Yu's textbook in Chinese (in the spirit of first three chapters of Cartan's "Elementary theory of analytic functions of one or several complex variables").
(2) We also refer to first three chapters of Stein-Shakarichi.
(3) If time permits, we discuss some supplementary topics, for example Lee-Yang's theorem on zeros.

Fall 2018-2019: Diferential geometry (an undergraduate course)

It is for junior math majors.
It covers elementary theory of curves and surfaces in 3-dim Euclidean spaces. We use Klingenberg's "Differential geometry" as the textbook.
